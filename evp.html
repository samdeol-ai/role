EVP — Vertical Head

The industry architect — connecting client transformation, AI propositions, delivery, and new commercial models into a scalable growth engine.

Executive Summary

The Vertical Head (example - HiTech & Semiconductor) owns the industry agenda, revenue growth, customer relevance, and transformation positioning for the vertical. In the AI era, this is a business-builder role connecting client priorities, industry trends, AI-led propositions, delivery capability, new commercial models, and thought leadership.

BCG estimates that agentic AI can create up to $200 billion in net-new demand for technology services over the next five years — but only providers who realign portfolios, talent, delivery, and commercial models will capture it.

Why This Role Matters in the AI Era

HiTech and Semiconductor at the Center of AI Growth

The semiconductor industry is being reshaped by AI chips, high-bandwidth memory, advanced packaging, data center demand, supply chain resilience, and edge AI. SK Hynix recently announced ~$12.85B investment in a new South Korea facility for AI memory and advanced packaging.

Enterprise Buyers Need Industry-Specific AI Partners

HiTech and semiconductor companies need partners who understand product engineering, cloud and platform modernization, ERP, supply chain, manufacturing systems, data platforms, and the silicon-to-software lifecycle.

Role Philosophy

A vertical leader should not only manage revenue. A vertical leader should shape how the company becomes relevant to the future of that industry.
  • Industry expertise is now a growth lever.
  • AI revenue quality matters more than traditional revenue volume.
  • Every account should produce industry learning that becomes a repeatable proposition.
  • Move from vendor to transformation partner — even if it challenges existing T&M models.

Core Responsibilities

  • Vertical Strategy — define growth strategy and identify priority segments (semiconductor design, manufacturing, equipment, cloud and data center, enterprise software, consumer tech, SaaS).
  • Revenue Ownership — own vertical revenue, growth, profitability, and revenue quality.
  • Client Transformation Leadership — engage senior client leaders and position the company in core business areas.
  • Industry Proposition Building — partner with COO and SMEs on AI engineering, product engineering, cloud, SAP/S/4HANA, data platforms, supply chain intelligence, agentic AI, and embedded AI.
  • Co-Creation and POCs — drive joint POCs that convert into scalable revenue.
  • Sales and Delivery Alignment — bring the right experts into strategic conversations.
  • Thought Leadership — publish POVs, host roundtables, represent the company at industry forums.

Strategic Themes for HiTech and Semiconductor

  • AI-Led Product Engineering — AI-assisted dev, test automation, productivity analytics.
  • Semiconductor Supply Chain Intelligence — demand forecasting, supplier risk, inventory.
  • Enterprise Application Modernization — SAP, S/4HANA, Salesforce, ERP modernization.
  • Cloud and Data Platform Modernization — cost optimization, lakehouse, real-time analytics.
  • Agentic AI for Enterprise Workflows — task-specific agents, governance, HITL.
  • Advanced Manufacturing Intelligence — predictive maintenance, yield, defect detection.
